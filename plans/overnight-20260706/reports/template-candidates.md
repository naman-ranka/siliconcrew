# Showcase Template Candidates

Curated for the SiliconCrew template gallery: designs a first-time visitor forks to
watch "spec ‚Üí RTL ‚Üí lint ‚Üí sim ‚Üí synth/GDS by an AI agent." Judged on visible behavior,
<500 lines RTL, deterministic self-checking TBs, metric-friendly sky130 synthesis, and
**redistribution licensing** (a public gallery, not an internal benchmark).

Sources surveyed: Tiny Tapeout archives (TT03‚ÄìTT08, web), the ASU Spec2Tapeout ICLAD-25
hackathon set (vendored locally), and the NVIDIA CVDP benchmark (local pipeline).

---

## RANKED TOP 6 (the gallery)

1. **7-Segment Seconds** (Tiny Tapeout TT05) ‚Äî *A single digit ticking once per second: the
   unmistakable "my chip is alive" hero tile.* ~60 lines, Apache-2.0, trivial adaptation, full GDS.
2. **Sequence Detector `0011`** (ASU p1) ‚Äî *The platform's canonical hello-world; ships a
   self-checking testbench and a proven sky130 GDS at 1.1 ns.* Smallest full spec‚ÜíGDS proof we have.
3. **8-tap FIR Filter** (ASU p9) ‚Äî *A pipelined signed DSP block that makes a hardware designer
   nod; deterministic convolution TB with published golden outputs, meets 8 ns.* Real-block area metrics.
4. **Traffic-Light FSM** (textbook / TT04) ‚Äî *A Moore FSM every engineer reads at a glance ‚Äî
   green‚Üíyellow‚Üíred on three LEDs, tiny area.* Best authored clean-room from spec (see licensing).
5. **LFSR Random Generator** (textbook / TT04) ‚Äî *Free-running pseudo-random stream: a perfect
   deterministic self-checking TB plus a noisy-LED demo.* Clean-room from spec; trivial synth.
6. **Simon Says Game** (Tiny Tapeout TT06, urish) ‚Äî *An interactive memory game with LEDs and
   tones ‚Äî the most memorable fork on the gallery.* Apache-2.0, easy adaptation, ~250 lines.

**Licensing red flags (headline):**
- **CVDP = do NOT ship as gallery templates.** NVIDIA-licensed, `no_commercial` split; problem
  text/harnesses are not ours to redistribute publicly. Keep it as an *internal benchmark* only.
- **Tiny Tapeout VGA Playground is GPL-3.0** (copyleft) ‚Äî replicate the *concept* from a fresh
  spec, don't fork the code.
- **ASU hackathon repo ships no LICENSE file** ‚Äî redistribution rights are unclear. Safest path
  for the textbook designs (p1, p9, traffic light, LFSR): treat the spec as inspiration and author
  our own spec + TB clean-room (these are ubiquitous textbook blocks; a 3-state FSM isn't copyrightable).
- **Only Apache-2.0 Tiny Tapeout designs are safe to fork verbatim.**

**Key insight for the bundle design:** a SiliconCrew template is *spec + TB + expected agent
trajectory*; the RTL is **generated by the agent**, not shipped. So for textbook designs, licensing
of the reference RTL is moot ‚Äî only the spec wording and TB need to be ours. This makes clean-room
authoring cheap and sidesteps every red flag above except CVDP (whose *harnesses* are the asset).

## Source 1 ‚Äî Tiny Tapeout (web)

Most visually compelling of the three sources. Every TT project wraps logic in a fixed top
`tt_um_<name>(ui_in[7:0], uo_out[7:0], uio_in[7:0], uio_out[7:0], uio_oe[7:0], ena, clk, rst_n)`.

**Adaptation cost of the `tt_um_*` wrapper:** for LED/display/FSM designs it's *trivial* ‚Äî rename
`ui_in`/`uo_out` to real ports, tie `ena=1`, convert active-low `rst_n`, delete the unused
bidirectional `uio_*`/`uio_oe` bus. It gets *annoying* only when a design routes real signals through
`uio_` (e.g. PWM audio) or must honour a fixed external pinout (VGA R/G/B/HSync/VSync bit map).

| Design (round) | What it does | Showcase | ~RTL | License | URL |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **7-Segment Seconds** (TT05 demo) | Divides clk to 1 Hz, counts 0‚Äì9 on a 7-seg | "It's alive" hero | ~60 | Apache-2.0 ‚úì | github.com/TinyTapeout/tt05-verilog-demo |
| **Simon Says Game** (TT06, urish) | Repeat a growing LED+tone sequence | Interactive, instantly understood | ~250 | Apache-2.0 ‚úì | github.com/urish/tt06-simon-game |
| **SN76489 PSG** (TT05, rejunity) | 3 square tones + LFSR noise, PWM audio | Actually plays chiptune | ~400 (near ceiling) | Apache-2.0 ‚úì | github.com/rejunity/tt05-psg-sn76489 |
| **VGA patterns** (vga-playground) | Animated stripes/rings/Conway on VGA | Most striking (full-screen color) | 50‚Äì150 | **GPL-3.0 ‚úó** | github.com/TinyTapeout/vga-playground |
| **LFSR RNG** (TT04) | Free-running pseudo-random stream | Great deterministic TB + LED noise | <100 | Apache-2.0 (verify repo) | tinytapeout.com/chips/tt04 |
| **Traffic-Light Controller** (TT04) | Moore FSM, timed green/yellow/red | Relatable FSM ‚Üí 3 LEDs | 80‚Äì150 | Apache-2.0 (verify) | tinytapeout.com/chips/tt04 |
| **LED Chaser / Sequencer** (TT04) | "Knight Rider" sweep, rate-selectable | Classic scanning bar | <100 | Apache-2.0 (verify) | tinytapeout.com/chips/tt04 |

Verdict: pick the two verified Apache designs (7-Segment Seconds, Simon) to fork; take
traffic-light / LFSR / LED-chaser as *concepts* and author clean-room (trivial textbook RTL).
Skip PSG (annoying uio audio, near size ceiling) and VGA (GPL) for a first gallery.

## Source 2 ‚Äî ASU Spec2Tapeout ICLAD-25 (local)

Strongest **flow-proof** source: each problem ships a clock target, a strict module signature, a
sample I/O vector, and the example even ships a real testbench + reference RTL + a proven
`6_final.odb` (sky130hd, `CORE_UTILIZATION=15`, `PLACE_DENSITY=0.6`). So "sim passes ‚Üí real synth
metrics ‚Üí GDS" is *known to close* at the stated clock. Downside: math/DSP blocks are less visually
relatable than an LED, and **p5/p7/p9 use SystemVerilog packed 2-D ports** (`[N-1:0][WIDTH-1:0]`) ‚Äî
iverilog needs `-g2012` and some flows choke, a real adaptation note.

| # | Design | Gist | Difficulty | Sim vs GDS | Hardware-designer nod? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **p1** | `seq_detector_0011` | 4-bit serial-pattern Moore FSM, 1-cycle detect pulse | Easy | Full GDS (proven, 1.1 ns) | Yes ‚Äî the clean canonical FSM |
| **p9** | `fir_filter` | 8-tap pipelined signed FIR, bit-growth accumulate | Medium | Full GDS (8 ns, relaxed) | Strong ‚Äî a real DSP block with golden convolution outputs |
| **p5** | `dot_product` | N=8 pipelined signed dot-product engine + `valid` | Medium | Full GDS (4.5 ns) | Yes, but abstract; packed-array ports |
| **p7** | `exp_fixed_point` | 2-stage e^x via 3-term Taylor, UQ fixed-point | Medium-hard | Full GDS (4.5 ns) | Niche; fixed-point rounding TB is fiddly |
| **p8** | `fp16_multiplier` | IEEE-754 half-precision multiply, round-to-even | Medium | Full GDS (9 ns, combinational) | Yes, but FP-rounding golden vectors are fiddly |

Verdict: p1 and p9 are gallery-grade (top 6). p5/p8 are good "serious block" second-tier.
p7 is the weakest showcase (most abstract, hardest TB).

## Source 3 ‚Äî CVDP (local pipeline)

The **NVIDIA Comprehensive Verilog Design Problems** benchmark, run through `cvdp-pipeline/` ‚Üí
`bench-orchestrator` and graded in the pinned `ghcr.io/hdl/sim/osvb` container. Problems are
identified by `datapoint_id` (e.g. `cvdp_agentic_DES_0001`) across categories (code-generation
`cid003`, design `DES_*`), each carrying a `context/` + a **cocotb harness materialized verbatim
from the dataset** ‚Äî the harness *is* the verification asset.

**Provenance / redistribution ‚Äî the blocker:** this repo ships **no CVDP data**; it reads a dataset
JSONL the user supplies, and use is governed by **NVIDIA's CVDP license + the OSVB image license**.
The split used is `no_commercial`. We **cannot redistribute the problem text or harnesses** as public
gallery templates. Honest recommendation: **CVDP stays an internal benchmark**, not a template
source. If we ever want a "cocotb-verified" gallery entry, author a fresh clean-room problem in the
CVDP *shape* (context + cocotb TB) rather than shipping a CVDP datapoint. (I did not enumerate
specific datapoints as candidates because doing so would imply we can redistribute them ‚Äî we can't.)

## Replication recipes for the top 3

### #1 ‚Äî 7-Segment Seconds (Tiny Tapeout TT05, Apache-2.0, fork or clean-room)
- **Spec outline:** parameterizable clock divider produces a 1 Hz tick; a BCD counter 0‚Üí9 wraps; a
  combinational 7-seg decoder drives `seg[6:0]`. Target ~50 MHz sky130 (divides trivially).
- **Top interface:** `module seven_segment_seconds(input clk, input rst, output [6:0] seg);`
  (add a `DIV` parameter so the sim can tick fast).
- **TB strategy:** set `DIV` tiny for sim; step through counts 0‚Äì9 and assert `seg` equals the
  expected decode for each digit; fully deterministic, self-checking, no waveforms needed to pass.
- **Flow depth:** **full GDS.** ~60 lines, meets timing with enormous slack; area is a handful of
  cells ‚Äî a clean, fast synth run that still produces real metrics.
- **tt_um adaptation:** trivial ‚Äî `uo_out[6:0]=seg`, tie `ena=1`, invert `rst_n`, drop `uio_*`.

### #2 ‚Äî Sequence Detector `0011` (ASU p1 ‚Äî best end-to-end proof)
- **Spec:** already precise (`p1.yaml`): serial `data_in`, synchronous active-high `reset`,
  `detected` pulses high one cycle when the stream matches `0011`; sample in/out vectors provided.
- **Top interface:** `module seq_detector_0011(input clk, input reset, input data_in, output reg detected);`
  (strict ‚Äî automated harness depends on it).
- **TB strategy:** the shipped `iclad_seq_detector_tb.v` drives the 16-bit vector
  `0001100110110010`, checks against `0000010001000000` on each posedge, prints PASS/FAIL and an
  error count ‚Äî a ready-made deterministic self-checking TB. (Author our own wording to sidestep the
  ASU-repo license question; the logic is a textbook FSM.)
- **Flow depth:** **full GDS, already proven** ‚Äî `example_problem/output/6_final.odb` exists at
  `sky130hd`, `CORE_UTILIZATION=15`, `constraint.sdc` at 1.1 ns. The single most bulletproof
  spec‚ÜíGDS trajectory in the whole set, and a great "lint fails ‚Üí fix ‚Üí sim passes ‚Üí synth" story.

### #3 ‚Äî 8-tap FIR Filter (ASU p9 ‚Äî the "serious block")
- **Spec:** parameterized N-tap signed pipelined FIR (`WIDTH=16, N=8`), multiply input samples by
  fixed coefficients and accumulate with bit growth `[2*WIDTH+$clog2(N):0]`; clock 8 ns (125 MHz).
- **Top interface:** `module fir_filter #(parameter WIDTH=16, N=8)(input clk, input rst,
  input signed [WIDTH-1:0] x_in, input signed [N-1:0][WIDTH-1:0] h, output signed [2*WIDTH+$clog2(N):0] y_out);`
- **TB strategy:** drive impulse-style stimulus `x_in=[1..8]` with coefficients `[1..8]`; assert
  `y_out` against the published golden convolution `[1,4,10,20,35,56,84,120]` (spec ships these).
  Self-checking and deterministic. **Note:** packed 2-D port `h` requires SystemVerilog ‚Äî
  compile with `iverilog -g2012`; some downstream tools want the ports flattened.
- **Flow depth:** **full GDS** at a relaxed 8 ns ‚Äî the multiplier/accumulator gives a *meaningful*
  area/timing report (unlike the near-empty FSMs), so it's the best tile for showing off real synth
  metrics that a hardware designer will actually scrutinize.
